# Pattern Study — 177 premium resume templates vs. the shipped `resume-designer` system

## 0. Method and a data-integrity note

Every count below is recomputed from the **per-record fields**, not from the per-source prose. That matters, because the prose miscounts its own data in at least six places: Adobe claims "11 of 16 sidebar" (records say 9), "14 of 16 one accent" (11), "11 of 16 photo" (12); Figma claims "8 of 14 sidebar" (5); Behance claims "11 of 24 skill bars" (9) and "~15 of 24 photo" (14). The per-source `surveyed` values sum to **175**, not 177 — `rezi-teal-standard` says 26 and lists 28. Where prose and records conflict, I use records.

Denominators are stated because they move the answer. **35 of 177 templates have `columns: unknown`; 71 have no observable `date_alignment`; 23 have no `accent_colors` field at all.** Rates computed over 177 understate every attribute.

| Field | Scored n | Observable subset used below |
|---|---|---|
| columns | 142 | 35 unknown (mostly Rezi 15, Enhancv 6, resume.io 4, Microsoft 4) |
| date_alignment | 105 | Enhancv 21 and resume.io 18 record none |
| accent_colors | 154 | Microsoft 4, resume.io 18, Enhancv "Crest" omit it |

## 1. WHAT RECURS

### 1.1 Structure (n=142 with known columns)

| Layout | n | % of known |
|---|---|---|
| two-column, any (47 sidebar + 33 content-split) | **80** | 56% |
| single | **36** | 25% |
| hybrid | **26** | 18% |

**Two-column is the majority of everything observable.** Single column is a quarter.

### 1.2 The two universals

Only two patterns have **zero recorded counterexamples in any source**:

1. **The name is the largest element and sits at the top.** Canva: "the single most invariant structure in the set" (18/18). Behance: "the largest element on the page in every single piece" (24/24). Novorésumé 12/12. Kickresume, Adobe, Figma, Rezi: same.
2. **ALL-CAPS section heading + a delimiter rule.** Called "near-universal heading grammar" (Behance), "the single most common treatment" (Figma), "universal — 12 of 12" (Novorésumé), "overwhelmingly ALLCAPS + full-width horizontal rule" (Rezi). Kickresume is the only dissenter (title-case; all-caps on 3/22).

### 1.3 Chromatic restraint is real and near-total

| accent_colors | n | % of 154 scored |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 29 | 19% |
| 1 | 93 | 60% |
| **≤1** | **122** | **79%** |
| 2 | 28 | 18% |
| ≥3 | **4** | **2.6%** |

The four templates exceeding two hues: *Modern CV Template Free* (6), *CV 2023 / Han Nguyen* (4), *+60 Resume/CV Templates* (4), Kickresume *Bubbles* (3). Three of the four are **collections or self-branded personal CVs, not products.** Maximalism is functionally extinct in shipped commercial work.

### 1.4 Skill rating bars are a legacy tail, not a norm

**32/177 (18%).** But 23 of those 32 come from two sources — Kickresume (14/22) and Behance (9/24). The modern cohort ships **zero**: Figma 0/14, Enhancv 0/21, Rezi 0/20, Standard Resume 0/8. Canva 1/18, Adobe 2/16.

And of the 32, **exactly one has a scale.** Novorésumé *Tech*: "REAL skill progress bars — horizontal fills with an axis tick scale." The other 31 are, in the corpus's own words, "unlabelled… no legend, no units, no baseline. Purely decorative rhetoric."

### 1.5 Dates have no winner (n=105 observable)

| Convention | n | % |
|---|---|---|
| left-under-title | **42** | 40% |
| inline (`Company \| 2022–2026`) | **34** | 32% |
| **right-aligned** | **21** | **20%** |
| sidebar/left gutter | 8 | 8% |

Right-aligned dates are a **Canva + Rezi** habit and nothing else: Canva 9/18, Rezi 5/5 rendered, Behance 3/19, Figma 4/11. **Adobe (0/15), Novorésumé (0/12) and Kickresume (0/22) ship zero right-aligned dates between them — 49 templates, no date rail.**

### 1.6 Design-led vs. ATS-led: they disagree on everything except type

Design-led = behance-dribbble (24) + figma-community (14) = **38**. ATS-led = rezi-teal-standard (28); Teal contributed **0** — Cloudflare 403 on every route, so "Rezi/Teal/Standard" is really Rezi (20) + Standard Resume (8).

| Attribute | Design-led | ATS-led |
|---|---|---|
| single column | **8/37 (22%)** | **6/8 (75%)** |
| two-column any | **19/37 (51%)** | **1/8 (13%)** |
| photo | **21/38 (55%)** | **1/28 (4%)** |
| skill bars | **9/38 (24%)** | **0/28 (0%)** |
| ≤1 accent hue | 25/38 (66%), max **6** | 28/28 (100%), max **1** |
| **icons** | **20/38 (53%)** | **20/28 (71%)** |
| allcaps + rule headings | near-universal | near-universal |

The ATS-led column-structure n is small (8 of 28 verified) because 20 couldn't be rendered — but the surveyor reports "**not one sidebar anywhere in an 18-template gallery**" for Rezi, and both ATS-led heterodoxies (Georgia's sidebar, Seymore's photo) belong to **Standard Resume, the source that doesn't market on ATS at all.** The source that *does* — Rezi, 20 templates — ships 0 sidebars, 0 photos, 0 bars, ≤1 hue.

**The one inversion is icons: the ATS camp out-decorates the design camp (71% vs 53%).** Rezi puts contact glyphs on 20/20 templates while its own docs ban two columns, progress bars, percentages and skills charts. This is the single most interesting disagreement in the corpus and it lands directly on a shipped ban. See §3.

## 3. REJECTED

| Banned | n in corpus | Reason — labelled honestly |
|---|---|---|
| **Sidebars** | 47/177 | **Measurement.** Side-by-side blocks merge at extraction; DOM order is not a lever in a PDF. |
| **Tables for layout** | Microsoft Support: "**Many templates use tables to streamline the layout**"; Enhancv *High Performer* ships "structured tables" under an ATS badge | **Measurement** — and "tables were the WORST performer" indicts the largest vendor in the corpus at its foundation. |
| **Skill bars/dots/stars** | 32/177 | **Measurement + information design, not taste.** 31/32 have no scale. Even Enhancv defends "pie charts" without defending their legibility. |
| **Photos** | **80/177 (45%)**; design-led 21/38 | **Convention (jurisdiction), not measurement.** Cost is real and should be said out loud: Standard Resume's *bestseller* is a photo template ("our most popular photo resume template"); Enhancv ships photo variants of 11. |
| **Text in images** | Kickresume *Nature* reverses name **and all contacts** over a photograph — and its alt text says "ATS-friendly" | **Measurement.** n≈2, correctly banned. |
| **Creative section names** | ~2/177 deviate; Canva and Kickresume both ship a fixed section vocabulary | **Convention — and the corpus is 175/177 with you.** This ban costs nothing. |
| **Justified text** | 1/177 (Canva *White Beige*, which "opens up ugly word gaps") | **Taste.** Well-founded, corpus-corroborated at n=1, but no measurement is claimed. Say so. |
| Company logos (implicitly, via text-in-images) | ~6 (Enhancv's four `-with-Logos` variants, Figma Coverse, Figma Simple) | **Measurement.** A logo image deletes the employer name from the text layer. Enhancv badges all four ATS-friendly. |
| Rotated spine labels | 2 (Dribbble *Carol Thompson*, Behance *Miguel Dias*) — both flagged "worth stealing" by the surveyor | **Measurement.** Lives in the margin (side-by-side) and rotated glyphs extract scrambled or not at all. Correctly refused despite the praise. |

### 3.1 The one rejection I would contest: decorative contact icons

**86/177 (49%) ship icons.** Novorésumé 12/12. Kickresume 20/22. **Rezi 20/20** — the source that explicitly forbids two columns, progress bars, percentages and skills charts in its own documentation, and still claims "All Rezi templates are verified ATS-friendly."

The brief gives **no measurement** for the icon ban, and the label should be **taste/caution, not measurement.** The actual hazard is narrower than the ban: **icon *fonts* extract as Private Use Area codepoints and inject junk into the contact line** (`\uf0e0 name@domain`). **Inline SVG extracts as nothing.** The shipped system already distinguishes text-in-images from text, so it knows the difference. As written, the rule bans the mechanism *and* the harmless one, and puts the system to the right of the strictest ATS vendor in the corpus on the one axis where that vendor didn't blink.

Recommendation: permit **inline-SVG** contact marks, gated behind a preset flag, with the text label retained. Zero text-layer footprint, and it closes the only decorative gap between the shipped system and Rezi.

## 4. MISSED

**Thesis: every structural premium move in this corpus is foreclosed by the measurement, and the shipped system correctly refuses all of them. What remains available is entirely typographic, semiotic, and packaging — and that is where the gaps are.** There are seven real ones, and one of them is a defect.

### 4.1 The overflow rule reproduces a defect the corpus is criticised for — fix it (free)

> "A record's left side must fit ONE line, else… Company drops to its own line past ~58 chars."

Applied **per record**, a four-job resume renders three entries as `Role — Company ......... 2021–2024` and the fourth as a two-line block. That is **two date conventions in one document** — which is exactly what the survey holds against Canva *Minimalist White and Grey*: *"Education dates sit above the institution while work dates are right-aligned — two date conventions in one document."*

**Fix: promote the drop rule to document scope.** If *any* record trips ~58 chars, *all* records adopt the dropped form. One line of logic; removes a defect the corpus already names.

Related: the shipped system chose the **minority** date convention (21/105, 20%) — one that requires a conditional. The plurality (left-under-title, 42/105) has no failure mode at all and costs one line per entry. Defensible (Rezi is 5/5 on-side), but 49 templates across three sources ship no date rail, and the ~58-char constant is the tell that this convention fights the text.

### 4.2 Paper size is unspecified — and it moves the 58-char constant

The system is **already region-aware** (photo ban scoped to US/UK/CA/AU) but says nothing about paper. US Letter (216×279mm) vs A4 (210×297mm) changes the measure by ~6mm and the depth by 18mm. **A load-bearing constant measured in characters cannot be paper-independent.** Corpus: Figma *Bebbble* "ships parallel A4 and US Letter artboards — the only file that shows both"; Canva *White Gold* is "A4 Printable"; Adobe *Beige Assistant* is "A4 rather than US Letter." **Add a paper axis and re-measure the threshold per measure**, or state that 58 is the A4 (narrower) figure and Letter inherits it conservatively.

### 4.3 Name micro-typography: the mixed-weight / two-tone split (8/177, free, unspecified)

The name spec is size, alignment, tracking. It says nothing about **splitting the name across two weights or two tones** — a device 8 templates use and the survey flags as stealable twice:

- Canva *Gray and White*: light given name + bold surname — "a recurring Canva trick for hierarchy **without color**"
- Canva *White Gold*: two-tone by colour — "a clean, cheap hierarchy trick **worth stealing**"
- Also Canva *Black and White Corporate*, Behance *Template Trails*, *Graphic Design Junction*, *Minimalist Resume Template*, Dribbble *Muhammad Bilal*, Figma *CV Template for Figma*

`SHASHANK REDDY` extracts identically whether or not the two words differ in weight. **Zero parse cost, zero ink, and it is the one place the shipped system can look art-directed at thumbnail scale.**

### 4.4 The monogram — the corpus's only answer to "what goes where the photo isn't"

The system bans photos and puts a colour band in their place. Four templates solve it instead:

- Dribbble *John Doe monogram*: "the **only piece in the survey to solve the photo problem rather than indulge it, and worth stealing**"
- Kickresume *Ladder*: initials roundel — "**worth stealing for photo-averse markets**"
- Standard Resume *Sidney* ("custom monogram color personalization") and *Georgia* ("oversized initials in the header, with color") — i.e. **the most typographically disciplined source in the corpus uses a monogram as its signature.**

It passes the shipped system's own test: a monogram beside the name merges to `SR Shashank Reddy` — **a line that stays true.** Add it as a masthead variant.

### 4.5 Type: Cambria is technically right and semiotically the most generic choice available

Cambria's selection (static, native lining + tabular figures) is the best-reasoned font decision in this entire review. It is also **the default serif of Microsoft Word 2007–2010.** It reads as *Word*. Standard Resume's entire product is managing exactly that connotation, and it reaches for **IBM Plex Serif** for its executive template (*Venables*) and **EB Garamond** for its "most elegant" one (*Cordova*).

Two concrete additions, both static:
- **IBM Plex Serif** or **Source Serif 4** as an alternate serif head — both default to lining figures with tabular sets available (verify per family before adopting; that's this system's own standard).
- **A monospace track.** Standard Resume *Keefer* differentiates on typeface alone: "*Technical, minimal, and unique*… a monospace font to stand out **without compromising the simplicity**." Mono is static, lining and tabular *by construction* — zero parse risk, ideal for the date rail, and a strong signal for engineering roles. **1/177 does this**, which makes it a differentiator rather than table stakes.

*(EB Garamond is not a candidate — old-style figures, same defect as Georgia. The shipped system already knows this; nobody else in the corpus does.)*

### 4.6 Heading variants that are visible at thumbnail scale

"Heading treatment" is a preset axis but only one value is specified. The corpus hands over a free library of parse-safe variants, and **one of them changes the gallery thumbnail**:

- **Centred all-caps flanked by rules both sides** — Rezi *Harvard*: "**Centered headings give it a unique look**" (Rezi's own words, and the *only* differentiator it claims for that template); Figma *ATS Friendly Resume*; Kickresume *Green*/*Basic*
- Rules **above and below** (Canva *Business School*); **short** rule rather than full-width (Canva *Gray and White*, Novo *Creative*); rule **above** only (Adobe *Orange Swiss*)
- Right-aligned auto-numbers `01 / 02 / 03` (Figma 🦇 — "a genuinely distinctive device"). **Auto-number it:** that file ships `01, 02, 04, 05` — it skips 03, which is exactly what happens when a section is deleted from a hand-numbered document.

### 4.7 Live hyperlinks in the PDF (2/177 mention it — which makes it a differentiator)

Figma *CV Template for Figma* markets "PDF Export **with Hyperlink**"; Figma *ATS friendly Resume & Cover Letter* uses "a stack of **underlined links**." That's it, out of 177. A real `/Link` annotation on the email/LinkedIn/portfolio is **free from `<a href>` in a Chrome print pipeline**, invisible to any parser, and is the single cheapest "this was made, not typed" tell available. If the pipeline already emits them, say so in the spec; if not, it is the highest ratio item in this list.

### 4.8 The artifact is a file; the corpus sells a set

Dribbble's *Roxin Mirons* ships a "3-piece set (resume p1 + p2 + letter cover) — **the standard Dribbble template-shop package**." *Carol Thompson* "ships with a matching cover letter." Figma bundles one with components. **A matched cover letter is prose, single-column, reuses the masthead, and has nothing to measure.** It is the largest packaging gap and it costs one template.

*(Also worth a look, lower confidence: a dedicated **achievements** block — resume.io *Header ATS/Shanghai*, "worth stealing." It isn't a creative section name; it's standard executive-resume practice. Whether the shipped section inventory has one isn't determinable from the brief.)*

### 4.9 One spec ambiguity worth closing

"ONE accent, **≤3 uses**" — the signature skeleton spends the whole budget: band (1) + reversed name (on the band) + accent employer names (2) + heading rules (3). **The accent axis can then change hue but not distribution**, which quietly shrinks the effective preset count. Worse: if "uses" means *instances*, the rule **forbids accent-on-every-employer on any resume with four jobs** — i.e. it forbids the survey's favourite Canva idea, the one the shipped system claims to have adopted. If "uses" means *element types*, say so.

## 5. THE COLUMN CONTRADICTION

### 5.1 The numbers

- **80/177 templates are two-column** (47 sidebar + 33 content-split) = **56% of the 142 with known structure.**
- **97/177 (55%) carry a blanket, catalogue-wide ATS claim**: resume.io (18, "created our templates with automated resume scanners in mind"), Novorésumé (12, "*all* our resume templates are entirely ATS-friendly"), Enhancv (21), Kickresume (22, "all templates are ATS-friendly"), Rezi (20), Microsoft (4, dedicated ATS gallery).
- **Of those 97 badged templates: 38 are two-column and only 20 are confirmed single-column** (29 unverifiable, 10 hybrid).
- **Among the 68 badged templates whose structure is known: 38 two-column (56%), 20 single (29%), 10 hybrid (15%). A blanket-ATS-badged template is ~1.9× more likely to be two-column than single-column.**
- **14 templates put "ATS" in their own product name.** 7 are single-column, **4 are two-column** — including one **literally named "Two Column ATS."**

### 5.2 The vendors contradict themselves, not just each other

Three sources publish ATS *guidance*. All three say single column. **Only one of them ships it.**

| Source | Its own guidance | What it ships under the badge |
|---|---|---|
| **resume.io** | /ats page: "**Use a single-column layout instead of columns or sidebars**" and "Avoid tables, text boxes, images, icons, and decorative lines" | 9 two-column + 1 hybrid badged, incl. flagship **Prime ATS** (two-column), **Precision ATS** (two-column), **Two Column ATS** |
| **Microsoft** | Blog: single-column, "**avoiding complex columns and tables**" | Support: "**Many templates use tables to streamline the layout**" |
| **Rezi** | Docs: "Two-column designs **often confuse screening software**… avoid progress bars or percentages… skills charts" | 0/20 two-column, 0/20 bars, 0/20 photo. **Claim and artifact agree.** |

Enhancv is the sole affirmative dissenter — and it is the **only source in 177 templates that produces a number**: *"98% vs 95% — this is how Enhancv's two-column resumes scored against single-column ones in our parse testing."*

**Read that number.** It is vendor-reported, unpublished, methodology-free, parser-unnamed — and **it concedes that two-column parses worse.** The only quantitative parse claim in the entire corpus supports the shipped rule while being deployed against it.

### 5.3 Does the corpus support or undermine the single-column rule?

**It supports it, and it does so from the pro-column side.**

Enhancv's defence is *"Built right, a two-column resume parses as cleanly as a plain one."* **"Built right" presupposes a lever.** The shipped measurement says that lever does not exist: *fixing DOM order does NOT help — a PDF has no DOM.* Enhancv is describing an HTML-era fix for a PDF-era problem. Nobody in the corpus tests the artifact they actually ship.

More precisely: **the corpus contains zero measurements and one anecdote.** The shipped rule is the only claim in this document backed by an experiment (flex, grid, CSS columns, `<table>`, two parser engines), and its worst-performing construct — tables — is the construction method of the corpus's largest vendor. Rezi arrived at the same conclusion by doctrine and its 20 templates are the corpus's proof-by-construction that nothing is lost structurally: **Rezi *Compact* is "engineered to accommodate 20% more content per page compared to the Standard template" via "clever layout and optimized spacing" — density without a second column.**

The rule stands. The corpus's 80 two-column templates are not counter-evidence; they are 80 instances of vendors optimising a thumbnail and badging the result.

## 6. VERDICT

**Yes — it would sit credibly beside this corpus, and against its nearest comparator it wins outright.**

Against **Rezi** (the closest thing to a peer, 20 templates): the shipped system matches Rezi on every axis Rezi cares about — single column, no bars, no photo, ≤1 hue, all-caps+rule, right-aligned dates — and then beats it at its own most creative gesture. Rezi's *Bold* is "**a single bold line of color at the top**"; the shipped masthead band is strictly a richer version of the same move. Rezi's docs argue; the shipped spec measures. **It out-designs Rezi on Rezi's terms.**

More broadly: **it is the only artifact in this 177-template corpus whose specification is written in units — points, ems, contrast ratios, character counts, named parser engines — rather than adjectives.** The corpus's vocabulary is "captivating," "eye-catching," "timeless," "the little black dress of resumes." Every shipped rule is falsifiable. Almost none of theirs are. It is also the only one that has noticed that **Georgia's old-style figures make a date column bounce** — which is why three sources (Adobe 16, Novorésumé 12, Kickresume 22 = 49 templates) ship no date rail and never explain why.

**Where it loses — specifically, and this is what a paying customer gets that this does not:**

1. **A photo.** 80/177 (45%). Standard Resume's *bestseller* is a photo template. Enhancv ships photo variants of 11. The ban is scoped to US/UK/CA/AU — which means **there is no SKU at all for DE/FR/CH/AT/NL/JP/BR and most of LatAm**, where a photo is convention, not indulgence. That's a missing product, not a design position.
2. **DOCX.** resume.io ships **12 of 18** templates as PDF **+ Word**, and the corpus notes the tell: "design ambition and Word export are inversely correlated." A PDF-only system with an excellent PDF-parse argument is answering a question the upload form didn't ask when it says *"Upload .doc, .docx, or .pdf."*
3. **A matched cover letter.** The corpus sells sets. This sells a file. (§4.8)
4. **Gold.** "Deep desaturated, ≥4.5:1 on white" mathematically excludes gold (~2.3:1 on white). Gold is the corpus's luxury cue — Canva *White Gold*, Dribbble *John Doe monogram* (navy+gold), Dribbble *Carol Thompson* (charcoal+gold), Novorésumé *Creative*, Adobe *Minimalist & Modern Lines*. Five templates reach for it; the shipped rule forbids it. That is a principled, correct, and **real** loss.
5. **Variety the buyer can see.** This is the sharpest criticism available. At gallery-thumbnail scale, the discriminable features are: column structure, sidebar, photo, colour band, name position. **The shipped system holds four of five constant and varies one.** Its 107 presets — skeleton × type × accent × heading × density — collapse to roughly **2–4 distinguishable thumbnails**; Canva's 18 templates produce about five, across four or five genuinely distinct skeletons. The corpus's variety is largely fake (Canva reskins one skeleton three times; Enhancv's "21 templates" is variant inflation over ~12–13 skeletons) — **but it is fake in the axis the buyer sees first, and the shipped variety is real in the axes the buyer sees last.** Nobody picks a resume by tracking. They pick it by silhouette. *Fixes exist and they're in §4.6 and §4.4: centred-flanked headings and a monogram masthead are the only two proposals here that change the thumbnail.*

**The one thing the shipped system should stop claiming as a differentiator:** the full-bleed colour masthead is presented as "the signature," but it is **the corpus's most common premium gesture** — ~15–18 templates reverse a name out of a colour band, and resume.io's stated creative formula is verbatim "a strong block of color at the top of the resume." It is executed here in the one way that survives extraction, which is a real achievement. It is not distinctive. The distinctive material is sitting unused in §4.3–4.5: a two-tone name, a monogram, a serif that isn't Word's, a monospace track.

**Net:** the engineering is the best in the room and the refusals are almost all correct. The gap is not rigour — it's that the system has spent all of its differentiation budget on things a PDF parser can see and none on things a human can see in 200 pixels. Seven concrete, parse-safe, thumbnail-or-typography moves are on the table in §4. **One of them (§4.1) is a defect fix, and one (§4.2) is a load-bearing constant that hasn't been checked against the other paper size.**
